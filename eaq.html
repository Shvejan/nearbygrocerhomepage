1. What Is Hybrid Marketing?

Hybrid marketing is a promotional based approach that comprises traditional marketing and digital marketing. Instead of focusing only on online platforms or in-person touchpoints, hybrid marketing effectively merges both approaches to create an even more effective and extensive outreach plan.

1.1 Definition

Because it is a new term, there is no consensus on the precise definition of hybrid marketing or whether it should be considered a synonym for integrated marketing. Broadly defined, though, hybrid marketing seeks to provide a consistent business identity across traditional marketing channels, such as TV and print, and online channels, such as websites and social media outlets.

2. How will that be effective?

Hybrid marketing is a new and evolving term and is sometimes referred to as omnichannel marketing or multi-channel marketing. The terminology is flexible, and so is the concept. Hybrid, omnichannel, and multi-channel marketing are fluid approaches to promoting a brand, product, or service through varied, yet cohesive methods.

5 What will be some of the best practices undertaken?
5.1How will you use consistent branding

Hybrid marketing messages are spread throughout multiple, varied platforms, but they should always feel like they are from the same place. Use consistent branding elements, colors, and fonts so audiences always recognize your brand

5.2 How will you plan to make the user experience seamless

Like branding, the hybrid marketing experience should also feel smooth and coherent. Each customer touchpoint should feel connected and united. While branding, messaging, and experiences should be consistent, they should also feel natural in their environment. Don’t take content from one platform and simply reuse it on another. When you prepare a hybrid marketing strategy, the message must remain consistent across all channels. This can mean a considerable amount of work. 12. What are the scheduling procedures to be Initiated?

To get the most out of a hybrid marketing strategy, the marketing materials should go live on every channel at the same time. This supports the consistency of brand and message. This does not mean you need to saturate all of the market channels with new marketing materials the same day. Just make sure to phase out any old marketing materials that do not reflect the new brand, color scheme and message for the business or product. Leaving old materials active in marketing channels, much like having different color schemes in different marketing channels, muddles the brand.
